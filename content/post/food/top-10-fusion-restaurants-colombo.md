Colombo's dining scene has undergone a remarkable transformation. Today, the city's best chefs are blending traditional Sri Lankan spices and ingredients with techniques from Japan, Peru, India, Italy, and beyond — creating a fusion food culture that's uniquely Colombo. From the legendary Nihonbashi to the brand-new Cinnamon Life restaurants, here are the top 10 fusion dining experiences in the city.

---

## Nihonbashi by Dharshan Munidasa

{{< figure src="/img/fusion/nihonbashi.jpg" title="Nihonbashi — Port City Colombo" >}}

The undisputed pioneer of Sri Lankan-Japanese fusion. Chef Dharshan Munidasa masterfully combines the freshest local seafood with precision Japanese techniques — think sushi-grade tuna from Sri Lankan waters, black cod marinated in local palm sugar, and wasabi grown in the hill country. Nihonbashi was the first Sri Lankan restaurant ever listed on **Asia's 50 Best Restaurants** (2013–2018), and Chef Dharshan was awarded the **Order of the Rising Sun** by the Government of Japan in 2023.

**Why it's on the list:** A true original that's been perfecting Sri Lankan-Japanese fusion for 30 years. Every dish tells a story of two culinary traditions meeting on one plate.

**⭐ Rating:** TripAdvisor 4.5+ | **Reviews:** 1,000+
**💰 Price range:** LKR 5,000 – 15,000+ per person
**📍 Location:** Plot 01, Port City, No. 5 & 6, Shop 01-12, Chaithya Rd, Colombo 01
**📞 Phone:** +94 77 936 0250
**🌐 Website:** [nihonbashi.lk](https://nihonbashi.lk/)
**🕐 Hours:** Lunch 12:00–2:30 PM | Dinner 6:00–10:30 PM
**🥇 Signature concept:** "Hero Ingredient" — local Sri Lankan ingredients meets authentic Japanese Washoku

*Photo: Nihonbashi*

## Ministry of Crab

{{< figure src="/img/fusion/ministry-of-crab.jpg" title="Ministry of Crab — Old Dutch Hospital" >}}

Asia's 50 Best Restaurants ranked — Ministry of Crab takes Sri Lanka's most beloved ingredient (crab) and presents it through a global fine-dining lens. Founded in 2011 by **Kumar Sangakkara**, **Dharshan Munidasa**, and **Mahela Jayawardene**, the restaurant operates with a daring **no-freezer policy** — all ingredients are ultra-fresh. The Pepper Crab and Chilli Crab are legendary, served in the beautifully restored Old Dutch Hospital building.

**Why it's on the list:** World-renowned. One of the most celebrated restaurants in Sri Lanka with thousands of verified reviews and multiple international locations.

**⭐ Rating:** 4.4 (Google) | **Reviews:** 5,400+
**💰 Price range:** LKR 10,000+ per person
**📍 Location:** No. 04 Hospital Street, Old Dutch Hospital Complex, Colombo 01
**📞 Email:** reservations@ministryofcrab.com
**🌐 Website:** [ministryofcrab.com](https://www.ministryofcrab.com/)
**🕐 Hours:** Lunch 12:00–5:15 PM | Dinner 5:00–9:15 PM (Closed Poya Days)
**🥇 Signature dish:** Pepper Crab | Garlic Chilli Crab | Crabzilla (2KG)

*Photo: Ministry of Crab*

## Indiya — Cinnamon Life

{{< figure src="/img/fusion/indiya.jpg" title="Indiya — Cinnamon Life, City of Dreams" >}}

A spectacular new addition to Colombo's dining scene, Indiya at **Cinnamon Life (City of Dreams)** offers Indian fusion at its most refined. The name is a portmanteau of "India" and "Yashvi" (meaning "glorious"). Chef Bhairav Singh presents a culinary journey through **Indian's 15-state cuisines** — from coastal Kerala to royal Rajasthan, all reimagined with contemporary techniques and presentation. It's Indian food, but not as you know it.

**Why it's on the list:** Cinnamon Life's crown jewel. Indian fusion at a level Colombo has never seen before.

**⭐ Rating:** New opening — highly anticipated | **Reviews:** Emerging
**💰 Price range:** LKR 10,000 – 22,000 per person (approx.)
**📍 Location:** Cinnamon Life, City of Dreams, Colombo
**🌐 Website:** [cinnamonlife.com](https://www.cinnamonhotels.com/cinnamon-life-city-of-dreams-sri-lanka)
**🥇 Signature dish:** 15-state Indian tasting menu

*Photo: Cinnamon Life*

## Paradise Road The Gallery Café

{{< figure src="/img/fusion/gallery-cafe.jpg" title="The Gallery Cafe — Paradise Road" >}}

Set within the iconic Paradise Road art gallery, this Colombo institution serves international cuisine with distinct Sri Lankan influences. Housed in the former office of renowned architect **Geoffrey Bawa**, the setting alone — tropical gardens, open-air dining surrounded by contemporary art — makes it a must-visit. The menu roams from Sri Lankan spiced nachos to wood-fired pizzas, pastas, and curries, all beautifully executed.

**Why it's on the list:** A beautiful marriage of art, ambiance, and creative fusion cooking. Perfect for brunch, lunch, or a relaxed dinner.

## The Lagoon — Cinnamon Grand

{{< figure src="/img/fusion/lagoon.jpg" title="The Lagoon — Cinnamon Grand Colombo" >}}

An Asian seafood fusion specialist with one of Colombo's most impressive live seafood counters. Pick your catch — crab, lobster, prawns, or reef fish — and the chefs prepare it Sri Lankan, Thai, Singaporean, or Chinese style. The Lagoon has been a Colombo institution for decades, consistently delivering some of the freshest seafood in the city with bold pan-Asian flavours.

**Why it's on the list:** The freshest seafood in Colombo prepared with pan-Asian flair. A seafood lover's paradise.

## Yoroko — Cinnamon Life

{{< figure src="/img/fusion/yoroko.jpg" title="Yoroko — Cinnamon Life" >}}

Embracing the essence of "Yorokobi" (joy and welcome in Japanese), Yoroko presents an unparalleled **Robatayaki dining experience** at Cinnamon Life. This modern Japanese restaurant seamlessly blends traditional Japanese aesthetics — think charcoal grills, premium sake, and omakase-style dining — with the sophistication of a world-class integrated resort. It's the closest thing Colombo has to a Tokyo-style robatayaki experience.

**Why it's on the list:** Colombo's most exciting new Japanese dining concept. The robatayaki grill brings theatre and flavour together.

### Tips for Fusion Dining in Colombo

- **Best time to go:** Most restaurants accept reservations — book ahead for weekends, especially at Nihonbashi, Ministry of Crab, and Cinnamon Life venues
- **Dress code:** Smart casual for most; fine dining at Nihonbashi, Ministry of Crab, and Indiya
- **Vegetarian options:** The Gallery Café, Kaema Sutra, and Graze Kitchen have excellent vegetarian menus
- **New openings:** Cinnamon Life (opened October 2024) — Indiya and Yoroko are worth booking early
- **Budget tip:** Lunch menus at Nihonbashi and The Gallery Café offer better value than dinner
- **Group dining:** King of the Mambo and Graze Kitchen are ideal for groups with varied tastes
